Bugatti

Bugatti is a renowned luxury automobile brand known for its high-performance sports cars. Founded in 1909 by the Italian-born French automobile designer Ettore Bugatti, the company has a rich history in engineering and innovation. One of the most iconic models produced by Bugatti is the Bugatti Veyron, which gained immense popularity for its exceptional speed and performance.



The Bugatti Veyron, first introduced in 2005, was the world's fastest production car at the time of its release. It was powered by an 8.0-liter quad-turbocharged W16 engine, generating an astonishing 1,001 horsepower. This remarkable power allowed the Veyron to reach a top speed of over 400 km/h (250 mph), making it a true engineering marvel.
